About me

I am a PhD Candidate in Statistical Science at Duke University, working with David Dunson.

I work on (Bayesian) statistics and (probabilistic) machine learning, especially in various structured settings (e.g. graph, convexity, physical constraints etc).

Beyond my core work on statistics and ML, I am also interested in high dimensional probability, spectral graph theory, convex geometry, as well as applications in neuroscience, chemistry, finance and imaging.
